Case: Building My First Portfolio Sitemap
The problem:
I didn't have a portfolio at all. I knew I had real skills to offer  customer support, VA work but I had no way to show potential clients what I could actually do for them.
What I did:
I started by asking myself who I actually want to help and how. That gave me a clear direction: SaaS founders who need calm, clear customer support. From there, I mapped out a lean 4-page sitemap ,Home, Case Studies, About, Contact  deliberately small, so a busy founder wouldn't have to click around too much. I also chose to lead with a proof statement instead of a bio, because I want people to see what I actually do before they read about who I am.
What came of it:
I pressure-tested the sitemap with Claude, and it pushed back on my structure  questioning whether four pages was too much clicking for a founder who's "wearing every hat" with no time to spare. It suggested a single long-scroll page might convert better. I hadn't considered that angle, and it's made me think seriously about testing a simpler structure before building out all four pages.
